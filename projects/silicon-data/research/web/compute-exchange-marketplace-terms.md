## Key facts

- **Legal entity:** Compute Exchange Inc. ("CEX")
- **Model:** Platform/marketplace — sellers list compute, customers buy. "CEX itself does not license any Compute through the CEX Services"
- **Auction mechanism:** Customers submit Bids, participate in Auctions with Bidding Periods. "No Cancel Period" makes bids irrevocable
- **Fees:** Fee Page referenced (separate). CX collects ~4% per transaction (from about page referral program)
- **Resale permitted:** Customers can resell unused compute through CX (minus consumed), paying reprovisioning fees
- **Market Data ownership:** "CEX retains all right, title, and interest in CEX Market Data" including pricing info, auction volumes, bid amounts, transaction timestamps, derived metrics
- **Governing law:** Delaware (Federal Arbitration Act), forum in Santa Clara County CA
- **Arbitration:** Mandatory binding individual arbitration via AAA, class action waiver
- **Key representation:** "CEX does not provide services subject to a regulatory license" (Section 5(f))
- **Contact:** support@tcex.com

## Critical regulatory statement

Section 5(f): "it acknowledges that CEX does not provide services subject to a regulatory license."

This is an explicit statement that CX claims no regulatory licensing is required. This is a diligence issue — whether forward contracts on GPU compute require CFTC or other regulatory oversight.
