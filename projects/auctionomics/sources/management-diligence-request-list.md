Tailored to **Auctionomics Inc. / OCX Group Inc. (OneChronos)** as of **April 9, 2026** and based on public information only. The public record shows a credible partnership between a Nobel-laureate-led market-design firm and a well-capitalized, SEC/FINRA-regulated trading venue operator, with proven technology in equities. However, the compute-market initiative is still at the announcement stage. The gating issues are: **(1) partnership legal structure, governance, and durability, (2) product specification, standardization framework, and regulatory pathway for compute forwards, (3) absence of any visible participants, transactions, or traction in compute markets, (4) dedicated team, budget, and timeline allocation versus the equities/FX expansion priorities, and (5) competitive timing versus Ornn and Silicon Data, which are further along in visible execution.**

## Management diligence request list

**Instruction to management:** please provide the following in a dataroom, with one folder owner per section, latest revision date on each file, and a short note indicating whether the file is final, draft, or board-only.

**Diligence sequencing:** populate all **P0 / gating** items first. No exclusivity, term sheet, or final IC recommendation should be considered until the P0 package is substantially complete and management interviews are held.

### A. Partnership structure and governance (P0 / gating)

1. **Partnership agreement and legal structure**

   * Full executed agreement between Auctionomics Inc. and OCX Group Inc. (or whichever entities are parties)
   * Legal form: joint venture, contractual arrangement, equity investment, IP license, or other
   * Duration, termination rights, and consequences of termination for the compute-market product
   * Whether a dedicated legal entity has been or will be formed for the compute-market initiative
   * Governing law, dispute resolution, and arbitration provisions

2. **Governance and decision-making framework**

   * Who controls product design decisions (auction mechanism, standardization, market rules)?
   * Who controls regulatory strategy and filings?
   * Who controls participant access, onboarding, and eligibility?
   * How are conflicts resolved between Auctionomics' consulting client interests and the compute-market initiative?
   * Board or committee structure, if any, dedicated to the compute-market venture
   * Milgrom's formal role, time commitment, and contractual obligations to the initiative

3. **IP ownership and licensing**

   * Which entity owns the compute-market auction design, standardization framework, and "building block" IP?
   * Is Auctionomics licensing IP to OneChronos, contributing it to a JV, or co-developing with shared ownership?
   * What happens to the IP if the partnership terminates?
   * Does Auctionomics retain rights to use the compute-market IP for other clients or projects?
   * Chain of title from individual contributors (Milgrom, Console Battilana, team members) to the entity

4. **Economic arrangements**

   * Revenue sharing, fee splitting, or commission structure between Auctionomics and OneChronos
   * Capital contribution obligations from each party
   * Cost allocation for development, regulatory, legal, and operations
   * Whether either party has made or received equity consideration

### B. Product specification and market design (P0 / gating)

5. **Product specification document**

   * The compute-market product as currently designed: what is being traded, how trades are matched, how prices are discovered, and how settlement works
   * The standardization framework (the "building block" referenced by Milgrom): what unit of compute is being traded, how heterogeneity (GPU type, memory, interconnect, location, power, availability) is represented, and how quality is verified
   * Auction mechanism: frequency, clearing method, bid format, combinatorial optimization approach, and winner determination
   * Whether the product is physical delivery, cash-settled, or hybrid
   * How bilateral forwards are structured: tenor, pricing, collateral, default provisions

6. **Market rules and participant framework**

   * Participant eligibility criteria (institutional only, or also commercial participants?)
   * Onboarding process, KYC/AML, and credit assessment
   * Market conduct rules, manipulation detection, and surveillance
   * Dispute resolution and default procedures
   * Fee structure and economics for participants

7. **Technology architecture for compute market**

   * How the existing Smart Market technology is being adapted for compute (vs. equities)
   * Latency requirements, matching engine design, and optimization algorithm specifications
   * Integration with existing compute infrastructure providers
   * Monitoring, surveillance, and audit trail systems
   * BCP/DR plans for the compute-market platform

### C. Regulatory pathway (P0 / gating)

8. **Regulatory analysis and outside-counsel memos**

   * Outside-counsel analysis of bilateral forwards for compute: are they exempt commercial forwards, or could they be characterized as swaps, futures, or securities under CFTC, SEC, or state law?
   * Identification of specific facts that would cause current forward structures to cross into regulated territory
   * Analysis of whether the auction platform itself requires registration as an exchange, ATS, SEF, or DCM
   * Analysis of whether the platform or either party would need to register as a broker-dealer, swap dealer, or futures commission merchant for compute-market activities
   * Any engagement with CFTC, SEC, FINRA, or state regulators regarding the compute-market initiative

9. **Regulatory roadmap**

   * Timeline from current state to operating marketplace, including all regulatory milestones
   * Contingency plans if the regulatory pathway requires registration, licensing, or structural changes
   * How the compute-market regulatory posture relates to OneChronos Markets LLC's existing ATS registration — shared or separate?
   * Any jurisdictional analysis for non-US participants (UK FCA, EU MiFID, Singapore MAS, etc.)
