# Second-Opinion Research Instructions: Ornn

You are providing an independent second-opinion diligence package on
**Ornn**.

## Audience

Write for an investment committee and phase-two diligence team. The work should
support skeptical underwriting, not promotion.

## Scope

- Use only public information unless private materials are explicitly provided.
- Preserve each material source as a separate research note with URL, fetch date,
  tool/query used, source type, reliability tag, and key facts.
- Maintain a source inventory table and a gap tracker.
- Do not draft conclusions until the evidence base is materially populated.
- Do not treat company claims as verified. Label them as company-stated,
  self-reported, third-party reported, regulatory, or unknown.
- Distinguish live products, announced plans, pilots, inferred traction, and
  public-record silence.
- Where evidence is absent, say "the public record does not show X" rather than
  filling the gap with speculation.

## Research Questions

1. What is the company/entity structure?
2. What product is actually live today?
3. What evidence supports traction, revenue, customers, usage, or distribution?
4. What regulatory, legal, licensing, or compliance issues are visible?
5. What methodology, data-rights, IP, benchmark, or technical-governance risks matter?
6. What team, control-function, key-person, and operating-capacity gaps exist?
7. What conflicts of interest or related-party issues are visible?
8. What financing, runway, capitalization, and burn questions remain open?
9. Who are the closest competitors, and what is this company's relative position?
10. What public facts would change the investment conclusion?

## Deliverables

### 1. Source Inventory

Provide a table with source name, URL, date, source type, reliability, topic
coverage, saved note filename, and status.

### 2. Gap Tracker

List each diligence question as resolved, partially resolved, unresolved, or
unanswerable from public sources. For every resolved item, cite the source note
that resolves it.

### 3. Investment Committee Memo

Include:

- Recommendation: clear gated recommendation, not a pitch.
- Company snapshot.
- What appears real today.
- Competitive context.
- Team and operating-model risk.
- Numbered core risks.
- Bottom line: what to do next, what would change the conclusion, and what would
  cause a pass.
- References with URLs.

### 4. Management Diligence Request List

Include:

- Tailored dataroom requests, not generic diligence.
- P0 / gating items separated from P1 / important items.
- First-call questions for management.

### 5. Management Response Evaluation Guide

Include:

- Score each core question on two tracks: management representation and proof
  status.
- Overall rating defaults to the weaker track.
- For each question, define acceptable, concerning, and unacceptable answers.
- Classify weak answers as competence/credibility failure, structural thesis
  failure, remediable build gap, or proof gap.

### 6. Second-Opinion Delta

After completing the independent analysis, compare your conclusions against the
existing Ornn package in `projects/ornn/`. Identify agreements, disagreements,
missing evidence, overstatements, understated risks, and any conclusion you
would change.

## Tone And Standard

- Analytical, concise, and source-driven.
- No promotional language.
- No unsourced factual assertions.
- Every major claim must trace to a source.
- Prefer direct public sources, regulatory materials, terms, filings, product
  docs, and reputable third-party coverage over press releases.

The key requirement is to produce an independently sourced underwriting view
first, then compare deltas only after the independent conclusion is formed. This
preserves the value of the second opinion and avoids merely rewriting the
existing memo.
